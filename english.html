what to do

ACTIVITIES AROUND CATALONIA

Girona

Girona is the capital of Catalonia's north-east region. Girona's well preserved old town with Saint Mary's cathedral, the City Walls Walkway and some Jewish heritage has been the inspiration and filming location for popular series such as Game of Thrones! The month of May is Temps de Flors and the city is filled with beautiful flower ornaments, which is the perfect excuse to venture yourself and explore the town.

Costa Brava

If you are looking for beer, paella and amazing views this is the place for you. During May, the beautiful towns of Costa Brava start getting warm enough for you to go for a swim or a dive and take in a full Mediterranean experience. If you're staying nearby Girona and love to walk or bike, you can plan for a day-trip excursion and cardio your way to a Costa Brava town. There are also public transport options to most of the towns.

Gastronomy

This is paradaise for foodies and globetrotters who enjoy to, literally, take-in the culture. From pa amb tomàquet to seafood platters, the meal choices are virtually untractable. The Catalan cuisine is rich on savoury dishes that have been sitting on the fire for a while, giving the meals a special homemade flavour. The sweets and desserts range from xuixos and popular catalan cocas to crema catalana and flams.

Barcelona

The capital of Catalonia with an extensive repertoire of interesting places for the artists, historians and football fans. There's way more to Barcelona than just a tour to Camp Nou. Dive in the food market La Boqueria, right next to La Rambla, and enjoy Barcelona's vibe and many of Gaudí's creations, such as La Sagrada Familia. The city outskirts offer more attractions such as Montjuïc Castle and Tibidabo's hill.

Banyoles

Èric's home town. Probably that's all you need to know to visit this Catalan gem (here we're talking about the town), but Banyoles has the most beautiful natural lake in Catalonia, home of the famous monstre de Banyoles (dragon's lake). It became popular as the rowing location for the 1992 Olympics. The town itself is surrounded by numerous parks such la Draga and Les Estunes (a mythical park full of magical fairies).

La Garrotxa

Arguably the region with the best cured meat and cheese in the whole Catalonia. Most of the region is a natural park, ideal for hikes or to enjoy from a bird's eye view in a hot-air balloon. The region features beautiful medieval towns such as Besalú's fort; La Fageda forest and Castellfollit de la Roca, a town of houses hanging on a cliff. You can follow these towns in La Garrotxa region making your way to the Pyrenees!
